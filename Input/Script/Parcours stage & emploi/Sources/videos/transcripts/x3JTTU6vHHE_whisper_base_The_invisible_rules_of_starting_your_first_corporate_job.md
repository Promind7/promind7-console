## Transcript (texte)

Visible rules of starting your first corporate job. When you get an assignment,

you should always explain back the instructions to the person giving them to you.

It'll help you understand the assignment better and also ensure that they

explain it correctly. And in general, you should just write everything down, including

assignment details. Not only is it helpful for memory, but it's also helpful for CYA.

Next, develop an email filing system, a lot of your job in a corporate context is honestly

finding emails on the social front, prioritize finding trusted mentors. They will be the ones

to help guide you through any particular, like, corporate situation you might have.

And when in doubt, ask the EAs, they know all. Managing upwards will make your life

infinitely easier and also help make sure that you don't cancel things because of work.

And then on the financial front, max out your retirement contributions and save

six, 12 months of an emergency fund as soon as possible. If you're hoping to save

up to become an artist or something, maybe save up even like two years of emergency funds.

Lastly, try to stay for at least a year. It just looks better.
